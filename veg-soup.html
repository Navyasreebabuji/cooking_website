Veg soup

This vegetable soup recipe is cozy, healing, and warming. It’s just the thing for cold nights, days when you’re craving a healthy reset, or any time you’re feeling a bit under the weather. I first started making it years ago, when I was bouncing back from a cold and had a ton of random veggies in the fridge. Using the ingredients I had in my pantry—vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, dried herbs, and vinegar—I transformed this jumble of vegetables into an amazing soup! Since then, I’ve made it again and again, with a different mix of veggies every time. No matter what produce I have on hand, this easy vegetable soup always comes out delicious.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

10 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : breakfast food

instructions

step 1

In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat.Add chopped onion and garlic, sauté for 2-3 minutes until soft.

step 2

Add chopped carrots, potatoes, zucchini, and green beans. Cook for 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Pour in the vegetable broth and the can of diced tomatoes (if using). Add salt, pepper, dried herbs, and the bay leaf. Increase heat and bring the soup to a boil.

step 3

Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let the soup simmer for about 20-25 minutes until the vegetables are tender. In the last 5 minutes, add the peas or corn and cook until they are soft.

step 4

Taste the soup and adjust salt, pepper, or herbs if needed. Remove the bay leaf if used.Serve hot with some bread on the side, if you like!

ingredients

1 cup of veggies

1 spoon of pepper

salt and water

tools

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
